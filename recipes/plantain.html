Plantain Porridge

Plantain is a popular Nigerian dish made from ground plantain, spices, and sometimes vegetables. It is typically served with a variety of sauces and is known for its unique flavor and texture.

Ingredients:

  • 2 heaps of unripeplantain
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of ground pepper
  • 1/2 cup of chopped onions (optional)
  • 1/2 cup of chopped vegetables (optional)
  • Water (as needed)

Steps:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the ground Bambara nuts, salt, and ground pepper. If using onions and vegetables, add them to the mixture.
  2. Gradually add water to the mixture while stirring until you achieve a thick, smooth batter consistency.
  3. Take a piece of leaf (such as banana leaf) and spoon a portion of the batter onto it. Fold the leaf to enclose the batter securely.
  4. Repeat the process until all the batter is used up.
  5. Place the wrapped Okpa in a steamer and steam for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until it is fully cooked and firm.
  6. Once cooked, remove from the steamer and allow it to cool slightly before unwrapping and serving.
